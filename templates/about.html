IgA Nephropathy

Berger's disease, also known as IgA nephropathy, is a kidney disorder that occurs when immunoglobulin A (IgA), an antibody that normally helps the body fight infections, builds up in the kidneys. This buildup leads to inflammation, which can hamper the kidneys' ability to filter waste from the blood effectively. Typically diagnosed in young adults, this condition can vary in severity, sometimes progressing slowly over years without symptoms, and in other cases advancing swiftly to kidney failure. Its implications are serious; while some affected individuals may live with stable condition for years, others might experience end-stage renal disease, necessitating dialysis or kidney transplantation. Moreover, the unpredictable course of Berger's disease can lead to significant anxiety and lifestyle changes for those diagnosed. Early diagnosis and management are crucial to slow the progression of the disease and maintain kidney function.

Oxford classification

The Oxford classification is used to score kidney biopsies in patients with IgA nephropathy (Berger’s disease), helping to predict the disease’s progression and guide treatment strategies. This scoring system is based on specific pathological features observed in kidney tissue sections under a microscope.

Mesangial hypercellularity

This score assesses the increase in the number of cells in the mesangial areas (parts of the kidney's filtering units). A score of M0 indicates less than 50% of mesangial areas have more than three cells, while M1 indicates more than 50% of the areas are hypercellular.

Endocapillary hypercellularity

This measures the presence of increased cellularity within the capillaries of the glomeruli that obstructs blood flow. E0 indicates no endocapillary hypercellularity, and E1 indicates its presence.

Segmental glomerulosclerosis

Glomerulosclerosis refers to scarring or hardening of the glomeruli. S0 denotes no segmental sclerosis, and S1 indicates its presence.

Crescents

Crescents are abnormal structures formed in the Bowman's capsule (part of the glomerulus) and indicate severe damage. C0 means no crescents, C1 means crescents in up to 25% of glomeruli, and C2 means crescents in more than 25% of glomeruli.

Tubular Atrophy/Interstitial Fibrosis

This scores the extent of damage and scarring in the tubular and interstitial areas of the kidney. T0 indicates no or minimal tubular atrophy/interstitial fibrosis (<25% of cortical area), T1 indicates moderate atrophy/fibrosis (25-50% of cortical area), and T2 indicates severe atrophy/fibrosis (>50%).
